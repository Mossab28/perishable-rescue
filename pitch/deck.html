The Problem — 02
▸ And the food that spoils is the food people need most
182,080
Alameda residents are food insecure — 11%, up from 9.6%.5 ACCFB's lived-experience figure: 1 in 4.3 Hunger concentrates in historically redlined East Oakland & Fruitvale.6
Demand drift
Agencies re-order bread out of habit → the desk "honors the last order" → fresh food expires while bread ships → the wrong food mix quietly locks into the highest-need blocks.
ROOT CAUSE — A decision bottleneck, not a food shortage. A standing order is a flawed signal; one operator can't weigh spoilage, equity and trucks at once under time pressure.

Deterministic — physics
Spoilage math, truck capacity, cold-chain, route feasibility. Same answer every time — safety is never reasoned away.
LLM — judgment
Who needs it most, correcting demand drift, multilingual notices. A single blurred prompt would silently drop one of these.
🧠
Learning layer — Risk & Need read past lessons before deciding; the system writes new patterns after every run. Never crashes: every step has a rule-based fallback. Run #1 is good; run #100 is exceptional.

Over half of U.S. food banks already run Ceres or Primarius — barcode-level, CSV/API-exportable7 — so our CSV adapter maps 1:1 to what they already export, and their barcodes are our lot-level traceability. It's a connector change, not an architecture change.
